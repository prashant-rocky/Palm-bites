Cookies Policy

Effective Date: August 5, 2025

This Cookies Policy explains how Palm Bites (“we”, “us”, or “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website https://palmbites.us.

By using our website, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this policy. You can manage your cookie preferences at any time through your browser settings or consent banner (if applicable).

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, mobile) when you visit a website. They help websites function efficiently and provide information to the site owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for basic site functionality, such as navigating pages and accessing secure areas.
  • Performance & Analytics Cookies: Used to understand how visitors interact with the site (e.g., Google Analytics). This helps us improve functionality and user experience.
  • Advertising Cookies: Used to deliver relevant ads to you on platforms like Google, Facebook, or Instagram. These may include retargeting/remarketing cookies to show ads after you’ve visited our site.
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your preferences, such as location or language settings (if applicable).

3. Third-Party Cookies

We may allow third-party services to place cookies on your device to assist with analytics and advertising. These providers may include:

  • Google Analytics
  • Google Ads (including remarketing)
  • Meta/Facebook Ads
  • Instagram Ads

These third-party cookies are governed by their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review them for more information on how your data is handled.

4. How to Manage Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Here are links to common browser support pages:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

You can also opt out of personalized advertising from Google by visiting Google Ads Settings, and from other participating ad networks via the NAI Opt-Out Tool.

5. Consent to Use Cookies

When you first visit our website, a cookie banner may appear asking for your consent to the use of non-essential cookies. You can change or withdraw your consent at any time through that banner or your browser settings.
